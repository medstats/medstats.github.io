Agentic Grand Rounds

Overview

Agentic Grand Rounds represents a frontier in medical education where multi-agent Large Language Model (LLM) orchestration is used to simulate complex clinical reasoning. Each case is a staged discussion between specialized AI roles:

  • Gemini (Resident): Initial case analysis, differential diagnosis, and proposed investigations.
  • Grok (Attending): Critical appraisal of the resident's plan, Bayesian refinement, and focus on "don't miss" diagnoses.
  • ChatGPT (Expert): Unifying diagnosis, key pivot identification, and high-yield teaching pearls.

This "Human-in-the-loop" framework (supervised by Anupam Singh MD) aims to sharpen diagnostic precision and explore the nuances of medical logic in a transparent, collaborative environment.

  • The Red Flag Embolus: A Multisystem Race

    A 64-year-old truck driver presents with sudden vision loss, ataxic gait, and splinter hemorrhages. A diagnostic race between high-stakes embolic syndromes where a striking "Red Flag" lab finding unmasks a silent cardiac threat hiding behind a preserved ejection fraction.

  • Bones, Stones, and Clonal Clues: A Hypercalcemic Dilemma

    A 78-year-old man presents with extreme hypercalcemia (17.1 mg/dL) and severe renal failure. A diagnostic maze where lytic bone lesions and a monoclonal IgM protein initially point to a classic hematologic malignancy, forcing the team to reconcile striking biochemical anomalies with hidden systemic pathology.

  • The After-Dinner Dip: A Postprandial Puzzle

    An 85-year-old woman with postprandial syncope and profound hypoglycemia. A diagnostic chase through common and rare etiologies where a subtle laboratory clue unmasks a surprising metabolic phenomenon.

  • Breathless and Weak: A Late-Onset Diagnostic Puzzle

    A 58-year-old woman presents with progressive dyspnea and dysphagia. A high-stakes diagnostic journey where cardiopulmonary failure and neuromuscular decline collide, leading the team through a maze of vascular, autoimmune, and rare protein-related pathologies.

  • Salt Cravings and Stolen Secrets: A Tubulopathy Tale

    Lifelong salt-craving and refractory hypokalemia presenting as a suspected eating disorder. A diagnostic journey where clinical persistence unmasks a genetic mimic.

  • The Heart in a Shell: A Case of Pseudo-Cirrhosis

    Progressive ascites and liver dysfunction in a young man. Explore the critical pivot where ascitic fluid analysis redirects the diagnostic frame from the liver to the mediastinum.

  • The Weight of a Cough: A Diagnostic Chase

    An elderly non-smoker presents with a unilateral pleural effusion and profound weight loss. Follow the stages as the case pivots from a suspected lung primary to a surprising systemic process.

  • Breathless at 26 Weeks: A Multidisciplinary Challenge

    A 29-year-old at 26 weeks gestation presents with acute exertional dyspnea and a history of connective tissue disease. A high-stakes management discussion balancing maternal diagnosis and fetal safety.

  • The Great Mimicker: Destructive Back Pain

    An 82-year-old woman with destructive L4-L5 disease and UMN signs. A diagnostic chase through suspected infection and molecular red herrings before a metabolic pivot reveals a surprising culprit.

  • The Lytic Lesion Mystery: Bone vs. Cancer

    A 44-year-old immigrant presents with a pathologic clavicle fracture and widespread lytic bone lesions. A diagnostic mystery where the radiographic appearance of metastatic cancer collides with striking biochemical anomalies, forcing a critical pivot in clinical reasoning.

  • The Persistent Pivot: Chronic Pain and High Stakes

    A 54-year-old immigrant presents with two years of worsening back pain and striking inflammatory markers (ESR >100). A diagnostic race between high-stakes infections and systemic inflammatory syndromes where therapeutic failure and a sudden surgical crisis unmask a hidden multisystem process.

  • The Beat Goes On: A Conduction System Mystery

    A 45-year-old man presents with a life-threatening electrical storm. A diagnostic journey where a past history of "asthma" and subtle clues in the cardiac conduction system serve as beacons, guiding the team through a maze of inflammatory and genetic cardiomyopathies to unmask a silent systemic threat.

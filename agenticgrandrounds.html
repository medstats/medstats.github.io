Agentic Grand Rounds

Overview

Agentic Grand Rounds represents a frontier in medical education where multi-agent Large Language Model (LLM) orchestration is used to simulate complex clinical reasoning. Each case is a staged discussion between specialized AI roles:

  • Gemini (Resident): Initial case analysis, differential diagnosis, and proposed investigations.
  • Grok (Attending): Critical appraisal of the resident's plan, Bayesian refinement, and focus on "don't miss" diagnoses.
  • ChatGPT (Expert): Unifying diagnosis, key pivot identification, and high-yield teaching pearls.

This "Human-in-the-loop" framework (supervised by Anupam Singh MD) aims to sharpen diagnostic precision and explore the nuances of medical logic in a transparent, collaborative environment.

  • Nemaline Myopathy

    A case of progressive weakness in a young child, exploring the diagnostic path of rare congenital myopathies.

  • Gitelman Syndrome & Anorexia Mimicry

    Lifelong salt-craving and refractory hypokalemia presenting as a suspected eating disorder, unmasking a genetic tubulopathy.

  • Constrictive Pericarditis: The Heart in a Shell

    Progressive ascites and "pseudo-cirrhosis" in a young man, highlighting the importance of high-SAAG/high-protein ascitic fluid analysis.

  • Pleural Effusion & Occult Malignancy

    Diagnostic reasoning for a moderate right-sided pleural effusion and constitutional symptoms in an elderly non-smoker.

  • UCTD & Exertional Dyspnea in Pregnancy

    Complex management of a 29-year-old at 26 weeks gestation with connective tissue disease and acute cardiopulmonary symptoms.

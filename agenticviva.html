Agentic Clinical Viva

Overview

Agentic Clinical Viva is an experimental assessment framework where a student's clinical case presentation is evaluated simultaneously by two leading AI models (ChatGPT and Gemini). This side-by-side viva explores:

  • Clinical Localization: The models' ability to identify the anatomical site of the lesion from history alone.
  • Physical Examination: Critical appraisal of the examination findings and their correlation with the suspected etiology.
  • Textbook Alignment: Each round is scored and referenced against standard medical texts like Kundu and Alagappan.

Supervised by Anupam Singh MD, this platform highlights the comparative strengths of AI in medical education and clinical reasoning.

  • The Progressive Weakness Viva

    A 47-year-old labourer presents with 4 months of progressive back pain, paraplegia, and bladder dysfunction. This agentic viva evaluates the AI models' ability to localise the anatomical site of the lesion and identify diagnostic milestones in a resource-limited setting.

  • The Yellowing Tide: A Jaundice and Distension Viva (Case A)

    A 47-year-old daily-wage labourer presents with 2 months of progressive jaundice and abdominal distension. An agentic exploration where AI models probe for etiological clues, quantify risk factors, and evaluate clinical signs of organ failure.

  • The Tapping Apex: A Breathlessness and Palpitations Viva

    A 42-year-old homemaker presents with progressive breathlessness, palpitations, and pedal edema. This agentic viva evaluates the AI models' ability to localise the cardiovascular lesion, assess its severity, and identify systemic complications.

  • The Ascitic Enigma: A Jaundice and Distension Viva (Case B)

    A 69-year-old female with diabetes presents with chronic jaundice and progressive abdominal distension. This viva probes a complex diagnostic dilemma, highlighting the AI models' role in navigating empirical therapy and differentiating potential etiologies.

  • The Itching Yellow: A Jaundice and Pruritus Viva

    A 56-year-old female presents with 12 weeks of progressive jaundice, generalized pruritus, and profound weight loss. This agentic viva explores the clinical logic of cholestatic syndromes, the application of Courvoisier's law, and the differentiation between benign and malignant biliary obstruction.

  • The Ascites with Fever Viva

    A 64-year-old male presents with 25 days of low-grade fever and 15 days of progressive abdominal distension and pain. This agentic viva evaluates the AI models' clinical approach to acute febrile illness with ascites, focusing on portal hypertension, spontaneous bacterial peritonitis, and early hepatic encephalopathy.

  • The Pallor with Splenomegaly Viva

    A 21-year-old female presents with 3 months of abdominal pain, fever, and excessive menstrual bleeding (polymenometrorrhagia). This agentic viva evaluates the AI models' ability to analyze chronic blood loss, evaluate prior bone marrow and transfusion records, and reason through secondary causes of moderate splenomegaly.

  • Mrs. R.K.: Progressive Dyspnea and Palpitations Viva

    A 61-year-old homemaker presents with progressive shortness of breath, palpitations, and cough. This agentic viva evaluates the AI models' clinical approach to cardiovascular symptoms, physiological basis of orthopnea, and cardiac rhythm disturbances with anonymized patient records.

  • Mrs. D.: Acute Headache, Vertigo, and Monoparesis Viva

    A 66-year-old female presents with 3 days of acute headache and vertigo, followed by sudden-onset left lower limb weakness (monoparesis) for 1 day. This agentic viva evaluates the AI models' localization of a suspected acute cerebrovascular event, interpretation of motor signs like pronator drift, and clinical navigation of stroke risk factors with anonymized patient records.

  • Do not rush to delivery just because the diagnosis is severe, unless maternal/fetal status forces that decision

Additional useful classification point

This is essentially:
- WHO Group 1 PAH
- likely CTD-associated
- with high-risk features / near WHO functional class IV physiology

Her cardiac output is especially ominous in pregnancy because normal gestation should increase output substantially; instead she is profoundly low output.

Prognostic significance

This is a maternal high-mortality condition, especially:
- second/third trimester
- delivery
- postpartum

The low CO and severe RV dysfunction are the most worrisome prognostic elements.

Teaching pearl

In pulmonary hypertension, the most dangerous number is often not the pulmonary pressure alone—it is the cardiac output.
A patient with very high PAP but preserved RV output may compensate; once the RV fails and cardiac output falls, risk of syncope, shock, and death rises sharply.

Round 1

User: A 78-year-old man was admitted with hypercalcemia and renal failure. Four months prior, he had a similar admission for dyspnea, cough, rib pain, flank pain, and one episode of hematuria. During that previous stay, examination showed basilar rales and a grade 2/6 systolic ejection murmur. Urinalysis was normal. Imaging at that time showed basilar atelectasis, mild congestive heart failure, a 1.3 cm subpleural nodule in the right middle lobe, and stable bilateral mediastinal/hilar lymphadenopathy with coarse calcifications. He improved with supportive care and was discharged with advice for follow-up imaging and cystoscopy.

Resident:

Hello, Chief Resident here.

Key findings include recurrent hypercalcemia, acute kidney injury (AKI), and a history of constitutional symptoms (rib/flank pain, hematuria). Imaging reveals stable calcified hilar lymphadenopathy and a subpleural nodule. The combination of hypercalcemia and renal failure in an elderly patient with bone pain and hematuria strongly suggests a plasma cell dyscrasia or malignancy.

Differential Diagnoses:

  1. Multiple Myeloma (45%): Explains hypercalcemia, AKI (myeloma kidney), and rib pain.
  2. Sarcoidosis (25%): Supported by bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy and hypercalcemia (1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D mediated), though calcifications suggest chronicity.
  3. Renal Cell Carcinoma (20%): Accounts for hematuria, flank pain, and paraneoplastic hypercalcemia.
  4. Hyperparathyroidism (10%): Common cause of hypercalcemia in this age group, though less likely to explain the systemic findings.

Recommended Tests:
1. Serum/Urine Protein Electrophoresis (SPEP/UPEP) with immunofixation and Free Light Chain assay: To screen for Multiple Myeloma.
2. Serum PTH and PTHrP: To differentiate between primary hyperparathyroidism and malignancy-associated hypercalcemia.
3. CT Abdomen/Pelvis: To evaluate the kidneys for masses (RCC) and assess for lytic bone lesions.
4. Serum 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D level: To evaluate for granulomatous disease.
5. Repeat Chest CT: To characterize the subpleural nodule and lymphadenopathy.
